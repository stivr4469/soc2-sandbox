# External Communication and Privacy Policy

## Purpose
This policy defines the standards for Marineso regarding external communications related to data handling, privacy practices, and security commitments. Adhering to SOC 2 CC2.3, this document ensures that all external stakeholders, including customers, regulators, and the general public, receive accurate and timely information about how Marineso protects their data. This policy establishes a framework for transparent communication, reinforcing our commitment to privacy and security across our digital operations and cloud infrastructure.

## Scope
This policy applies to all external communications directed toward customers, regulatory bodies, and the public. The primary owners of these communications are the Customer Success, Legal, and CISO departments. All employees, contractors, and third-party partners who may represent Marineso in an external capacity or handle data within the stivr4469/compliance-sandbox GitHub repository and AWS (us-east-1) environments are subject to these standards.

## Definitions
- **External Communications:** Any information or data shared with stakeholders outside of the Marineso organization, including but not limited to customers, regulatory agencies, and the general public.
- **Data Minimisation:** The principle of limiting personal data collection to what is directly relevant and necessary to accomplish a specified purpose.
- **SLA (Service Level Agreement):** A defined commitment between Marineso and an external party regarding the timeframe and quality of service or response.
- **PII (Personally Identifiable Information):** Any data that could potentially identify a specific individual, managed primarily through Okta (trial-7222443.okta.com).

## Privacy Commitments
Marineso is dedicated to maintaining the highest privacy standards for all data processed within our ecosystem. Our commitments include:
- **Data Minimisation:** We collect only the data that is strictly required for the stated business purposes and service delivery.
- **Purpose Limitation:** Data is never used for purposes beyond the scope disclosed to the user at the time of collection.
- **Retention Limits:** Data is stored only as long as necessary and is deleted according to our published retention schedule.
- **Data Subject Rights:** We honor all access, rectification, and deletion requests, ensuring they are fulfilled within 30 days of receipt.

## Security Disclosure Standards
To maintain trust and accuracy, all public security statements, including blog posts, press releases, and security advisories, must undergo a rigorous review process. No security-related information may be published without the joint approval of the CISO and the Legal department. This ensures that all disclosures are technically accurate, legally sound, and consistent with our security posture as monitored via the compliance dashboard at http://localhost:8000. All logs related to access control via Okta are considered sensitive and their disclosure is strictly regulated.

## Vulnerability Disclosure
Marineso maintains a responsible-disclosure programme to encourage the security community to report potential vulnerabilities. Security researchers are directed to send their findings to security@marineso.com. We commit to a response Service Level Agreement (SLA) of 5 business days for all initial vulnerability reports. This programme covers all Marineso-managed assets, including our AWS (us-east-1) infrastructure and the stivr4469/compliance-sandbox codebase.

## Customer Breach Notification
In the event of a confirmed data breach, Marineso adheres to a strict notification timeline. Affected customers will be notified within 72 hours of breach confirmation. The content and the communication channel used for these notifications must be approved by the Legal department. We also ensure that regulator notification occurs in compliance with applicable laws and industry standards. All incidents triggering this process are escalated through the Slack #compliance-alerts channel for immediate response and coordination.

## SOC 2 Report Sharing
Our SOC 2 Type II report is a sensitive document that reflects the maturity of our internal controls. This report is shared with current or prospective customers only under a signed Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA). Every request for report disclosure must be individually approved by the CISO. This process ensures that our security audits, which cover our use of Okta and AWS, are shared responsibly with authorized parties.

## Regulatory Inquiries
Marineso maintains a centralized process for handling inquiries from regulatory authorities. All such inquiries must be routed to the Legal department within 24 hours of receipt. The CISO provides the necessary technical input regarding our security controls and cloud infrastructure. No employee is authorized to provide a direct response to a regulatory body without explicit approval and oversight from the Legal department, ensuring that our compliance status as shown on http://localhost:8000 remains accurate.

## Responsibilities
The following roles hold key responsibilities under this policy:
- **CISO:** Responsible for approving all security-related disclosures and individual SOC 2 report sharing requests.
- **Legal:** Approves all regulatory responses, breach notifications, and public-facing legal statements.
- **Customer Success:** Manages direct customer requests and inquiries, ensuring responses are handled within the agreed SLA and in alignment with approved messaging.
- **All Employees:** Required to report any potential security or privacy concerns immediately to the Slack #compliance-alerts channel.

## Evidence for Audit
To demonstrate compliance with CC2.3, the following evidence will be maintained and provided during audits:
- **Communication Logs:** Records of external communications regarding security and privacy, including approvals from CISO and Legal.
- **Vulnerability Reports:** A log of all reports sent to security@marineso.com and documentation of responses within the 5-day SLA.
- **Breach Notification Records:** Documented evidence of customer and regulator notifications following a confirmed breach.
- **NDA Records:** A list of approved SOC 2 report disclosures and corresponding signed NDAs.

## Enforcement
Maintaining the integrity of our external communications is critical to Marineso’s reputation and compliance status. Any unauthorized external security disclosure by an employee or contractor will result in an immediate escalation to the CISO and Legal department. Violations of this policy may lead to disciplinary action, up to and including immediate termination of employment or contract, and potential legal action if NDA terms are breached.

## Review Cycle
This policy is reviewed at least annually to ensure continued alignment with SOC 2 standards and regulatory requirements. An out-of-cycle review may be triggered by a significant data breach, the introduction of new regulatory requirements, or a material change to Marineso’s data-processing activities or infrastructure, such as major changes to our AWS (us-east-1) environment or Okta (trial-7222443.okta.com) configuration.
